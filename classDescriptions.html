NRA Basic Pistol

The NRA Basic Pistol orientation course is designed with the novice shooter in mind. This course provides a hands-on introduction to the safe handling, storage, and proper orientation for a specific action type. This course runs approximately 5 hours long and includes classroom and significant range time learning to shoot.

Learning Objectives

  • the NRA's rules for safe gun handling
  • pistol parts and operation
  • shooting fundamentals and marksmanship
  • pistol cleaning and maintenance
  • and will be introduced to continued opportunities for skill development.
Every student participant will receive the Basics of Pistol Shooting Handbook, Winchester/NRA Marksmanship Qualification booklet, a Basic Practical Rocker patch, and a course completion certificate.

Required Equipment

  • Positive Mental Attitude
  • Pistol: 9mm or larger caliber (loaners available for nominal charge)
  • Not less than a 4 lb trigger pull (we will check)
  • Light range bag or sturdy backpack
  • 100+ rounds of Ammunition
  • 2+ magazines or speed loaders
  • Cleaning kit
  • Hearing protection
  • Eye protection
  • Baseball style cap or any cap with a brim
  • Sunscreen
  • Suitable clothing for the weather (no low cut necks, wear comfortable shoes and dress in layers)
  • Lunch

NRA Range Safety Officer

The NRA Range Safety Officer program was developed in response to the demand for a nationally-recognized range safety officer certification. NRA Range Safety Officers are people who possess the knowledge, skills, and attitude essential to organizing, conducting, and supervising safe shooting activities and range operations. NRA Range Safety Officers must be 21 years of age or older. The Range Safety Officer course consists of: Range Safety Officer's roles and responsibilities, range standard operating procedures, range inspection and range rules, firearm stoppages and malfunctions, and Range Safety Briefings which include emergency procedures. Candidates must also receive 90 percent or better on the written exam.

Red Cross Adult First Aid/CPR

Would you know what to do in a cardiac, breathing or first aid emergency? The right answer could help you save a life. With an emphasis on hands-on learning, our First Aid/CPR course gives you the skills to save a life. All courses align with OSHA’s Best Practices for Workplace First Aid Training Programs. Certification is issued upon successful completion (2 year certification).

Refuse to be a Victim® sponsored by Tri Valley NRA Members Council

The Refuse To Be A Victim® seminar is a non-firearm program from the NRA about how to stay safe. You can minimize your risk of becoming a crime victim by increasing your awareness and learning about many personal safety options. You will learn about elements of the criminal mind and the most current safety strategies to use while you're at home, work, school, shopping, traveling, etc. Also we will show you some very simple and effective bare hand self-defense techniques. Our current everyday challenges require defenses against high-tech intrusion, identity theft, sexual predation and other intelligent well-planned criminal tactics. Experts agree that you will be better off if you rely on your own carefullycrafted safety plan which covers your entire lifestyle. Your tuition includes handbook, certificate and valuable supplements.
